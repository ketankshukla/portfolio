title: My Experience With Bloc So Far
---

So far my experience with Bloc has been incredible. I have found myself bound by different experiences depending on the kind of checkpoint that I was working on.

I was able to zip through the foundation modules, thanks mostly due to the Codecademy prep work that we were asked to do before the course started.

Also, when I got started, about a couple of weeks into my program I was offered to upgrade to the new Rails curriculum, which they hadn't completed at the time they offered the students to sign up.

They said that by the time we got to those checkpoints, they would be completed as they were toward the end of the Foundation group of checkpoints.

Seeing myself speeding through it, I managed to catch up with the beta checkpoints and my mentor recommended that I get started on one of the projects and then come back to the Foundation checkpoints and finish the new ons once they were completed.

So I got started on my first project which was Blocitoff. Initially I had to go back and forth to refer to the notes on how I got some of the tasks done in the Foundation group but eventually I was able to pace myself on getting things done without doing too much reference.

I realized that I was able to learn the control-flow and navigation of code a lot better when I didn't depend on scaffold generation and controller and model generation. When I created these files one at a time, I understood their place in the project a whole lot better.

So I took this mentality and carried it over to the other two projects, Blocmarks and Blocmetrics and found it a lot easier to understand piece by piece.

Currently, I am just about to complete my portfolio and start working on my Capstone project and I couldn't be more thrilled having taken this program with Bloc.

I think the mentorship by far is the best part of this program, and I have learnt a lot from my mentor Chris Beck. He has given me ideas on better ways to write code and also better ways to prepare myself when I'm done with the program and out looking for a job.
After my Capstone project is done, I'll be working on the Job Prep module, which I'm really looking forward to.

So I have to say that my time with Bloc so far has been exciting, challenging and thrilling!
